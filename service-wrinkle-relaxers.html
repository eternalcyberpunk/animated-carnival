What it is

Small protein. Big difference.

Wrinkle relaxers — commonly known by brand names like Botox, Dysport, and Xeomin — are injectable neuromodulators that temporarily relax the muscles responsible for dynamic wrinkles. The ones that show up when you frown, laugh, or squint.

They don't change your face. They soften the expressions that, over years, etch themselves into your skin. Done well, nobody can tell you've had anything done. Done poorly, you know immediately. At Boon, we err on the side of "nobody can tell."

Jessie has administered over 50,000 units of neuromodulator across a decade of practice. She uses only FDA-approved products sourced directly from manufacturers.

Is this for me?

Who wrinkle relaxers are (and aren't) for.

Great fit if you...

  • Have dynamic wrinkles (lines that appear when you make expressions)
  • Want to prevent lines from deepening over time
  • Experience tension headaches or teeth grinding
  • Are bothered by "11s," crow's feet, or forehead lines
  • Are generally healthy and over 18

Probably not if you...

  • Are pregnant or nursing (we'll wait)
  • Have deep, static wrinkles at rest (filler may be a better fit)
  • Have a neuromuscular condition
  • Want permanent results (these wear off in 3–4 months)
  • Want a completely frozen, expression-free look (we don't do that here)
Jessie's approach

Why my Botox doesn't look like Botox.

I use less than most injectors. That's the short version.

The industry standard is to dose aggressively — 20–25 units for the forehead, 20 for the 11s, 24 for crow's feet. That's how you get the frozen, shiny forehead look that makes Botox obvious.

I start with 60–70% of that for first-timers, sometimes less. You come back at two weeks and we assess. If we need more, we add. That's how you keep expression in your face while still softening the lines.

I'd rather have you come back and add five more units than put in too much and have you wait four months to feel normal again.

Over a decade, I've learned where everyone's muscle patterns are different — and that the "standard map" most injectors are trained on doesn't fit most faces.

Questions

Honest answers.

Will I look frozen?
Not if Jessie's the one injecting. The frozen look happens when too much product is placed in the wrong muscles. We dose conservatively and preserve natural expression — you should still be able to raise your eyebrows and furrow your brow, just with less of a line.
Does it hurt?
Minor pinching, like a mosquito bite. Treatment takes 15 minutes. We use very small needles and ice if you're sensitive.
How long does it last?
Typically 3–4 months for first-timers. Most clients settle into treatments every 4 months. It wears off gradually — you'll notice movement coming back before the lines return.
Is there downtime?
Essentially none. Small red marks at injection sites for an hour or two. You can work out, go back to work, and wear makeup after four hours. Avoid lying flat for four hours and avoid heavy exercise the same day.
How much will I need?
Impossible to say without seeing you. Most women need 20–40 units for their first visit. Men usually need more — they have stronger facial muscles. Jessie will estimate at your consultation and write it into your treatment plan.
Can I start young as "prevention"?
Sometimes yes, sometimes no. "Preventative Botox" is a real thing for people who already have prominent dynamic lines in their mid-twenties. For most people under 28 with no lines at rest, the answer is "not yet." Jessie will tell you honestly.
