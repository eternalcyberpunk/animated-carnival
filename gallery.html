Why we're doing it this way

Aesthetic medicine runs on trust.

The default for medspa websites is wall-to-wall before & after photography — often unlicensed, often stock, sometimes lifted from other practices. We've seen clients' photos end up on websites they never authorized. It's industry-standard, and it's wrong.

At Boon, we treat your face as yours. If we ever show your photograph, it's because you signed a consent form specifying exactly where and how. Which means building a real gallery takes time.

We'd rather have a quiet page for a while than a full page of photographs we didn't properly earn the right to use.

So while this page fills in, the best way to see what Jessie's work looks like is to sit in the chair. Consultations are free and she's happy to walk you through real examples privately — including cases specific to whatever you're considering.

Our gallery principles

How we'll build this properly.

When the public gallery goes live, these are the rules it'll follow — the same ones we apply to Jessie's private case binder today.

01

Written consent, always

Every client whose photo appears here will have signed a specific consent — not a blanket waiver buried in intake paperwork. Scope, duration, and usage are all spelled out.

02

Same conditions, every time

Before and after photographs are taken in the same room, the same distance from the camera, the same lighting, and the same expression. No staging, no filters, no retouching.

03

Honest timing

We'll show you when the "after" was taken — 2 weeks, 4 weeks, 6 months — so you can judge the actual timeline of results. No before-breakfast-vs-after-makeup tricks.
